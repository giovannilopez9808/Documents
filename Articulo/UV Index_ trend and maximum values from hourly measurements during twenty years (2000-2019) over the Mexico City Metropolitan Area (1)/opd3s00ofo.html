On the another hand, aerosols may reduce the insolation and inhibit the photolysis rate due to smog formation \cite{Dickerson_1997}. A modelization of actinic flux applied to Mexico City, showed a decrease mainly due to the aerosol presence \cite{Palancar_2012}. Likewise, a reduction in the theoretical values of nitrogen dioxide photolysis rate was also associated to spectral actinic solar flux attenuated by aerosols \cite{Castro_2001}. Consequently, UV irradiance provides plenty information about the atmospheric components that attenuates this irradiance. 
In spite of multiple efforts to monitor and characterize the air quality in the whole Mexico City basin, few studies have specially focused on global solar radiation \cite{Qui_ones_2014,Matsumoto_2014,valdes1991}. In the mid-1990s, two studies about UV solar irradiance measurements in the Mexican Valley in periods shorter than 3 years were published. One of them suggested a significant attenuation on UV intensity due to tropospheric ozone, comparing measurements in downtown and outskirts of the Mexico City \cite{Acosta_2000}. Other study reported a reduction on UV radiation flux (around 25.6% at noon and 50% in the afternoon) lower than in Colima, a pollution-free city in the same latitude of the Mexico City \cite{Galindo_1995}. Up to now, these works have been the only ones carried out in this topic in the MCMA.
