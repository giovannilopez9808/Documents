This \(H_{er}\)value to produce erythema was established as the Minimal Erythemal Dose (MED), which has been widely used as a primary and preventive measure of skin damage. Many studies have been dedicated for the numerical estimation of MED varying with respect to skin phototype \cite{Meinhardt2008,Miller2012,MacKie_2000}. The CIE proposed the Standard Erythemal Dose (SED) with a value of 100J/m2 as an erythemally weighted unit dose, independent of skin phototype \cite{organization2014}. So, \(H_{er}\) can be calculated for each skin phototype, in units of SEDs. In this work, the lower limits defined by Fitzpatrick for erythemal skin, under UVB radiation for each phototype, were taken as reference in terms of SED \cite{Fitzpatrick1988}.  
The skin cancer is one of the most damaging effects of the long-term solar exposure and a malignant neoplasm, very common in most regions the world. The WHO published on its webpage that the prevalence continues to increase. Likely in United Mexican States there is a sub-register, since the majority of skin cancers are not cause of death \cite{jm2011}. While melanoma is a primarily risk on fair skins, the long-term exposure produces melanoma in latino population, mainly those working in outdoor conditions \cite{Rouhani_2008}.
