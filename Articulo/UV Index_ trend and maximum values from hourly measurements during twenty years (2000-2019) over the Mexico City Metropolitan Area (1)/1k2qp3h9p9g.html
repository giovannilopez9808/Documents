In order to estimate the change percentage per year in the period 2000-2019 we use\[\epsilon\left(\%\right)_{2000-2019}=\frac{{m_ {X}}\cdot100\%}{\overline{X_{(2000-2019)}}}\]where \(m_ {X}\) is the slope of  the linear fit with subscript depending on the variable and \({\overline{X_{(2000-2019)}}}\) the respective mean in all period. From daily \(UVI_{\max}\), the monthly averages (\(\overline{UVI_m}\)) and Standard Deviations (SD) were calculated each year. The Simple Moving Average function from Pythonwas applied on UV Index values in periods of 3 months and a linear regression along the twenty years (Figure \ref{443547}a). The resulting slope \(m_{UVI}=0.0048\) was divided by 9.07 the total average (\(\overline{UVI_{(2000-2019)}}\))  to estimate the percentage trend per year:
