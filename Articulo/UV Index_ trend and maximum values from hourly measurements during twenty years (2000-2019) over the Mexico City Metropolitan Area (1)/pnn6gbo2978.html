To compare the ground-base measurements at solar noon, the UV Index measured by OMI-Aura/NIVR-FMI-NASA were mapped. Figure \ref{674611} represents the satellite UV Index data from 2005 to 2019 (there was no previous data in the analyzed period). As can be seen, the levels are higher than the monthly averages shown in Figure \ref{443547} (where the UVI maximum values barely exceed 11). From the middle of January to mid-November, the satellite UV Index varies from 8 up to about 15 (\(UVI_{\max}=14.9\)). In particular, values equal or larger than 11, corresponding to the Extreme qualification for the UV index given by WHO, are commons from April to September.
