Unfortunately, skin cancer is a malignant neoplasm very common in the world. WHO published on its webpage that the prevalence continues to increase. Likely in United Mexican States there is a sub-register due to the majority of skin cancers are not cause of death \cite{jm2011}. Furthermore, various authors agree that prevention and timely diagnosis continue to be the main strategy to reduce the incidence of skin cancer \cite{a2016,Pinedo_2009,b2006}. While melanoma is a primarily risk on faire skins, the long-term exposure produces melanoma in latinos mainly those working in outdoor conditions \cite{Rouhani_2008}.
