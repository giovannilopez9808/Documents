UV Index (UVI) is an indicator related to the intensity of the solar UV radiation at the Earth’s surface and the risk of suffer sunburn\cite{Madronich_1997}. The reddening effect by unprotected exposure to sun, depends on skin phototype. Fitzpatrick classificated the VII phototypes: Albino (I), Light/Fair (I-III), Beige/Pardo (III–V) and Dark/Ebony (V–VI). The International Commission on Illumination (CIE) established the erythemal action spectrum (or erithemic sensitivity) of the human skin using as reference the phototype II \cite{organization2014}. The erythemal irradiance from 250nm to 400nm is defined as:\[E_{er}=\int_{250nm}^{400nm}E_{\lambda}\cdot S_{er}\left(\lambda\right)d\lambda\]
where Eλ is the solar spectral irradiance in units of W/(m2 ·nm) and Ser is the erithemic sensitivity defined by the CIE. According to the World Health Organization (WHO) the UVI can be calculated by the following formula:
\[IUV=k_{er}\cdot E_{er}\]
