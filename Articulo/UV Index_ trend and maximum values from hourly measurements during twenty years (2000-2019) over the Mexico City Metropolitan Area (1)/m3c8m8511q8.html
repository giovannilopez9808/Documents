Measurements analysis
UV Index: We developed a code in Python to process the hourly averages UV Index, 24 hours per day, 365 days a year, along two decades. Hereinafter, we refer to hourly averages simply as UV Index unless otherwise specified. The script identify the maximum UV index and ignore the empty spaces and invalid values product of the maintenance of the equipments. The algorithm creates a matrix with the maximum UV Index (\(UVI_{\max}\)) values sorted by date, hour, years. Thereupon, it calculates the averages according to the scan on the different time scales as well as \(UVI_{\max}\) recorded in all network and all period. In particular, the values from 11h to 14h LT were isolated to computed the daily averages around solar noon and the absolute maximums.
Aerosol Optical Dept: The AOD340  data were selected the annual averages with 7 months or more of continuous measurements.
Criteria pollutant: In particular, the values from 11h to 14h LT were isolated to computed the daily averages at solar noon and the absolute maximums.
For this purpose, we analyse the Radiative Cloud Factor measurements provides by the Ozone Monitoring Instrument (OMI). The Cloud Factor comes from dataset of OMTO3 v8.5, Collection 3 and L2 quality. The Cloud Factor (dimensionless ) is represented in scale from 0 to 1 for the cloudless days and overcast sky, respectively.
The 1,461 observations that were selected from TOMS-EP/NASA correspond to the years 2000 to 2003 and OMI-Aura/NIVR-FMI-NASA data to the years 2004 to 2019, both dataset covering the complete time series of the ground-measurements.

Results and Discussion

The daily \(UVI_{\max}\) were selected between 11:00 h-14:00 h LT from all ground-based measurements over MCMA. A counter as an extension of the code was executed, to quantify these maximum values and the percentage of the days in the period 2000 to 2019. The results indicated that, from a total of 7,305 days of continuous measurements, the daily  \(UVI_{max}\) reached values between 6 and 9 the 62.37% of the days (Figure \ref{929259}). The highest UV Index values were in the 13-14 range, being their total percentage frequency less than 1%.
