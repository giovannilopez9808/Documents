Ultraviolet (UV) range is only a small part of total incident irradiance and plays a key role in atmospheric photochemistry \cite{Logan_1981,Brasseur_1998}. UV radiation on the pathway through atmosphere is affected by gaseous absorbers mainly the ozone as well as NO2 and SO2. An increasing of the surface ozone concentration is also generated by a strong UV photon flux interacting with anthropogenic precursor emissions \cite{Lei_2007}. On the another hand, the aerosols may reduce the insolation and inhibit the photolysis rate for smog formation \cite{Dickerson_1997}. A modelization of actinic flux applied to Mexico City, showed a decrease mainly due to the aerosol presence \cite{Palancar_2012}. Likewise, a reductiong in the theoretical values of nitrogen dioxide photolysis rate was also associated to spectral actinic solar flux attenuated by aerosols \cite{Castro_2001}. That is why UV irradiance provides plenty information about the atmospheric components that attenuates this irradiance. 
In spite of multiples efforts to monitor and characterize the climatology in whole basin, few studies have specially focused on global solar radiation \cite{Qui_ones_2014,Matsumoto_2014,valdes1991}. In the mid-1990s, two studies about UV solar irradiance measurements in the Mexican Valley in periods shorter than 3 year were published. One of them suggested a significant attenuation on UV intensity due to tropospheric ozone, comparing measurements in downtown and outskirts of the Mexico City \cite{Acosta_2000}. Another authors reported a reduction on UV radiation flux (around 25.6% at noon and 50% in the afternoon) was lower than in Colima, a pollution-free city in the same latitude \cite{Galindo_1995}. Up to now, these works have been the only exclusively ones carried out in this topic in the MCMA, despite there is a vast scientific literature about UV irradiance that has contributed to knowledge the global climatology \cite{Luccini_2006,Herman_1999,Kalliskota_2000,Madronich_1993,Herman_2010}. Even more, there is no investigations about UV Index behavior along the time in this megacity.
