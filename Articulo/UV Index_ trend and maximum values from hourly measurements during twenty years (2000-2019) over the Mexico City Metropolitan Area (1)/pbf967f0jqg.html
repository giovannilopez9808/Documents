Based on ground measurements analysis under all sky conditions we make an assessment of the values reached around solar noon. From daily \(UVI_{\max}\), the monthly averages (\(\overline{UVI_m}\)) and Standard Deviations (SD) were calculated. The Simple Moving Average function from Python was applied by quarter and a linear fit along the two decades was determined (Figure \ref{443547}a). The linear equation has a slope \(m_{UVI}\) of 0.06 per year and the y-intercept (year 2000) has a UVI value of 8.5. For the sake of representing an annual behavior, the average, the median, SD, maximum and minimum of the UV Index were obtained (Figure \ref{443547}b). The maximum and minimum monthly mean of the UV Index were 10.6 (in May) and 6.5 (in November), respectively.
