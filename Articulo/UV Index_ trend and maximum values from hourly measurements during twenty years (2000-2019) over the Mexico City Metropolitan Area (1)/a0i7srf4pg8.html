Base on measurements analysis under all sky conditions we make an assessment the values typically reached around solar noon. From daily \(UVI_{\max}\), the monthly averages (\(\overline{UVI_m}\)) and Standard Deviations (SD) were calculated. The Simple Moving Average function from Pythonwas applied on monthly by quaters and a linear fit along twenty years (Figure \ref{443547}a). The linear equation had a slope \(m_{UVI}\) of 0.06 and 8.5 is the y-intercept. For the sake of simplify a annual behavior, the average, the median and SD, the maximum and minimum of the UV Index were obtained (Figure \ref{443547}b).
