UV Index (UVI) is an indicator related to the intensity of the solar UV radiation at the Earth’s surface and the risk of suffer sunburn \cite{Madronich_1997}. Fitzpatrick classification is conform by six phototypes or skin colors (from pale to dark) correlating the UV radiation sensitivity \cite{kukita1974}. The International Commission on Illumination (CIE) established an action spectrum for erythema (or erithemic sensitivity) of the human skin using the phototype II from Fitzpatrick classification \cite{organization2014}. According to the World Health Organization (WHO) the UVI can be calculated by the following formula:\[UVI=k_{er}\int_{250nm}^{400nm}E(\lambda,t)\cdot S_{er}\left(\lambda\right)d\lambda\]
where \(E\left(\lambda,t\right)\) is the solar spectral irradiance in units of W/(m2 ·nm),  \(S_{er}\left(\lambda\right)\) is the erithemic sensitivity defined by the CIE and \(k_{er}\)is a factor equal to 40 m2/W.
