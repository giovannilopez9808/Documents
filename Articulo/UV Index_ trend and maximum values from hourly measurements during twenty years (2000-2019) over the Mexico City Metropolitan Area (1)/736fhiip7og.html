The WHO standarized the UVI scale as: low (0, 1 and 2), moderate (3, 4 and 5), high (6 and 7), very high (8, 9 and 10) and extreme (11 or more) \cite{2002} even, values of UVI equal to 20 or more, have been recorded in La Quiaca, Argentina \cite{Cede_2002}. An extended recommendation of the WHO version is unfolding UVI values after of 11 and modifying the colour scale \cite{Zaratti_2014}. That suggestion could be more appropriate for the Mexico City region due to UVI reaching high values almost all year \cite{Tanskanen_2006,Herman_2010}.  The skin reddening caused by solar radiation depends on:  intensity of the source, skin phototype and exposure time.
